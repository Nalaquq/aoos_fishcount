# PVC Pole Mount Build Guide

A 2" Schedule 40 PVC pole is the recommended mounting structure for this system.
It is lower cost than a survey tripod, locally repairable in the field, and
height-adjustable for different river sites.

## Materials

| Item | Spec | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| 2" Schedule 40 PVC pipe | 10ft sections x2 | ~$16 |
| 2" Schedule 40 PVC coupler | Cemented joint | ~$3 |
| 1.5" galvanized steel pipe, 3ft | Driven stake base | ~$8 |
| 2" PVC end cap | Top of pole | ~$2 |
| 1/4"-20 stainless bolt, 20mm | Camera mount | ~$2 |
| Paracord 550, 50ft | Guy wires | ~$8 |
| 6" galvanized tent stakes x6 | Guy wire anchors | ~$6 |
| PVC cement and primer | Coupler joints | ~$8 |

**Total: ~$53**

## Assembly

### Base
Drive the 1.5" galvanized steel pipe 2–3 feet into riverbank soil using a
hammer or driving cap. Slip the 2" PVC pole over the stake — the stake acts
as an inner mandrel anchoring the base while the PVC provides height.

### Pole
Join two 10-foot sections with a cemented coupler at the desired height.
Apply PVC primer to both surfaces, let flash 30 seconds, apply cement,
push together and hold for 30 seconds. Allow 1 hour before loading.

### Camera Mount (Top)
Drill a centered hole through a 2" PVC end cap. Thread a 1/4"-20 stainless
bolt through from inside the cap, secure with a locking nut inside.
Cement the cap to the top of the pole. The exposed bolt thread accepts
a standard ball head → Arca-Swiss plate → Pelican 1120.

### Guy Wires
Attach three paracord lines to the pole using PVC pipe clamps at 80–90%
of pole height. Run lines at 120° intervals to driven stakes 8–10 feet from
the base. Use truckers' hitch or taut-line hitch for adjustable tension.
Guy wires are mandatory — without them wind load will cause enough vibration
to degrade image sharpness at 30fps.
